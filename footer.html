HOW WE STARTED

On 26 August 2016, NGO First Human Rights Foundation was found by socialist activist, as a non-governmental, non-profit and non-political organization. The organization was established as the will of citizens to participate actively in the Protection of Human Rights, as well as to take an active role in the civil society development through protection of civil Liberties. First Human Rights Foundation is registered with the Central Government of India with registeration no. 166151/16. FHRF is dedicated to the protection of human rights and promotion, upliftment of economically backward people, peace, national integration by communication between people. FHRF is dedicated to spreading the value of human rights to every part of India. Legal Services:- FHRF provides legal service to the weaker section of society. FHRF organizes Lok Adalat for amicable settlement of disputes. FHRF focuses on education, health, legal aid, employment, child labor, women empowerment.

OUR APPROACH

FHRF strives to bring together all the people who believe in democracy and human rights values in – social , political, economic areas. FHRF shall be a non-political, non-government organization. FHRF promotes health, education, legal awareness amongst the society and works for structural changes to fulfill the eco-social and political aspirations of all people and the protection of their ethics as well as religious and cultural rights. FHRF educates people about their fundamental rights and civil liberties. FHRF strives to the independence and impartiality of the judiciary freedom of the Press and other media and the right to have access to information. FHRF focuses on national and international problems in its depth and complexity. FHRF strives for promoting a secular, democratic and just society and people’s participation in the decision-making process at every level of society where people can raise their voices on different issues.

What is the GRE?

The GRE (Graduate Record Examination) is administered by the ETS. The GRE assesses your aptitude in math (basic arithmetic, algebra, geometry, and data analysis) and verbal (reading comprehension, sentence equivalence, and text completion.) The verbal section of the GRE includes advanced vocabulary words, so it is important to devote study time to expanding your vocabulary.

How Should I Study?

Prior to your GRE, you should plan to set aside 4 - 10 weeks of dedicated study time. Remember to go through practice materials and hone your skills! But, there are other things you can do (months) in advance, such as studying difficult vocabulary words! You can also spend time reading scholarly articles (like those in the Economist and Scientific American) to get used to reading and digesting challenging technical writing.

Register for the GRE

The GRE is administered year-round in more than 160 countries and at testing centers across the United States. Before you begin registration, be sure to review this checklist provided by the ETS to ensure you’re ready to rock!
